Amazing movies and tv series

Sherlock (BBC)

In this adaptation of Sir Arthur Conan Doyle's books, Benedict Cumberbatch presents my favourite interpretation of Sherlock Holmes (as a character) ever. The show perfectly balances chaos and control, with everything feeling fully planned on the producers' sides. All of the actors in this show have made great choices with their characters, and all make me want to laugh and cry simultaniously.

Dead Poets Society

Dead Poets Society is (along with the Prestige and the dnd movie) one of my absolute favourite movies. It presents a theme of new vs. old, tradition vs. new thinking, conformity vs. revolution, and this is apparent in almost every aspect of the movie. From the music to the plot itself to the message it conveys, this contrast always shows up in some way or another. It feels utterly planned and thought through, and the amount of detail is insane. Here too, the actors do a great job, but in a grounded, regular sort of way. It all makes me feel like I'm in the movie myself.

Nevermoor

Sooo Nevermoor isn't actually a screenplay (yet), but it would be really, really, really cool as as one! (If done right) I think it would have had to be an animated series- and not a movie. A movie would be way too little time, and this great story absolutely deserves not to be rushed. The reason I think it should be animated is partly the amount of cgi that would be needed to make it live action, and partly that I think a more childish, caricaturish style would fit the universe and the characters really well, and portray them in a way I think live actors couldn't.
